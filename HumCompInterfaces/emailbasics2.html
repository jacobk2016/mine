Email Lessons (Basics)

Email Interface


Every email interface will be a little different, depending on your service provider. However, to make it easier on new users, most email interfaces follow the same basic formula. For this lesson, we decided to make an example using Yahoo Mail. If you look below, you'll see a diagram of an email interface, with the various parts labled.



1. These are your email folders. You can find the messages you've recieved, sent, drafted, deleted or blocked by selecting these different folders. These folders are explained more in the next lesson.


2. These are your email controls. In order to use them, you select emails that you want to change and then use the controls on them. Primarily, the "Delete" function is important. You can select an email and then press the delete button to send it to your trash folder.


3. This is a read email. You can tell the email is read because the sender (see #7 below) is not bolded. By clicking on it, you can open the email and see it again. When you click on it, it pulls up the email and displays it for you. You can return to your inbox by clicking the inbox button in your folders. (More about this in the next lesson)


4. This is an unread email. You can tell the email is unread because the sender (see #7 below) is bolded. By clicking on it, you can open the email and see it. When you click on it, it pulls up the email and displays it for you. You can return to your inbox by clicking the inbox button in your folders. (More about this in the next lesson)


5. This is your subject column. This is where a person will tell you what the subject of their email is.


6. This is the description column. A preview of your email content will appear here.


7. This is your sender column. The person or business who sent you the email will have their name here.


8. This is the button you use to create a new email.


In this example picture, the inbox folder is open. All of the folders in the folders area look similar inside.


Next, I want to show you what an email looks like when you open it. It will typically open inside the same interface as the rest of your email, but it looks slightly different. Check out our diagram below for an example.



1. This is the sender line. It tells you who sent the message.


2. This is the to line. It tells you who the message was sent to.


3. This is the subject line. It tells you what the message is about (See #5 in the first diagram).


4. This is the content area. It contains everything that is in the email. This is usually a combination of pictures and text, and can look like anything. A lot of content will look like a letter, but in this case it looks closer to a web page. Email attachments will usually appear below this area, but this example does not have any attachments. (See "Email Attachments" lesson)
