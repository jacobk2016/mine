Email Lessons (Basics)

Your Folders


In the last lesson, we talked briefly about email folders. Email folders are important because they organize all of your sent, recieved and drafted messages. This makes it easier for you to find what you are looking for. To effectively use your email, you need to understand how these folders work.


You'll usually find your folders organized on the left side of the page, sort of like this. There are many folders in email, and the amount of folders depends on your service provider. For now though, we're going to just focus on the Inbox, Drafts, Sent, Spam and Trash folders.


When you've looked at the example picture, and you're ready to learn about the folders, scroll on down and start reading. Be sure to take your time so you understand each folder and all of it's impotant functions.


Inbox - This is where you'll find all of your read and unread recieved messages. It's usually the folder that is automatically open when you log into your email account. When you want to go to your inbox, click the word "Inbox" in your folder listing.


Drafts - This is where you'll find all of your written and saved messages that have not been sent yet. (More about saving drafts in the "Send an Email" lesson). When you want to go to your drafts, click the word "Drafts" in your folder listing.


Sent - This is where you'll find all of the messages you have successfully sent to another person. Once an email is sent, there is no taking it back. However, you may want to see a record of what you've sent. When you want to go to your sent messages, click the word "Sent" in your folder listing.


Spam - This is where all of your "spam" email goes. Spam email is email that is spoofy, contains excessive advertisements or is from suspicious senders. If your email service provider recognizes something as spam, the email will be put in this folder. However, some things are wrongfully put in spam so it doesn't hurt to check this folder when you're expecting an email and you haven't recieved it yet. When you want to go to your spam messages, click the word "Spam" in your folder listing.


Trash - When you delete an email, it goes to this folder. So, if you delete something from any other folder by accident, you can recover it by going here. You should try to empty this folder regularly because over time it will become very full. However, once a message is deleted from this folder, it is deleted permanently. Make sure you have not deleted anything by accident and sent it to this folder before you empty it. When you want to go to your deleted messages, click the word "Trash" in your folder listing.



Now that you've learned how to use and navigate your email folders, you're ready to learn about setting up contacts. When you're ready to move on, click "Next Lesson" below. If you would like to review the email interface in our last lesson, you can click "Previous Lesson" instead.
