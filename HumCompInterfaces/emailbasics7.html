Email Lessons (Basics)

Email Etiquette


Email, like any other form of communication, has certain etiquette rules you should try to follow at all times. In this section, we're going to talk to you a little bit about that etiquette.

Email is usually treated like a letter sent through the mail. When you write an email, it should have a salutation at the top, such as "Dear Mr. Smith,". This salutation should be as formal as appropriate for your relationship with the person you are sending it to.


The salutation should be followed by some space, and then the body of the email. This can be as long or as short as you need. Often, people prefer to read shorter emails so unless it is a big announcement, you may want to keep intermediate communication to the point.


After your body, you'll want to leave some more space, and then sign off the email with "Regards, MyName" or something of that nature.

There are a lot of ways to annoy people with email. For example, chain emails with jokes or silly images may be entertaining to you, but not to the person you are sending it to. Try to use email for communication purposes, and not to pass on stories or pictures you get from other people. For the most part, a good rule of thumb is to keep in mind what your recipient may or may not like, and try to cater to that. If your email is for business purposes, you should treat the communication as formally as possible and avoid joke emails entirely.
